03Sources & methodology

How we estimate. Your ZIP determines your regulated utility (TDU) — CenterPoint, Oncor, AEP Texas, or TNMP — whose delivery charges are included in every price shown[3]. Usage starts from typical Texas homes of your size (studio ≈ 600 · 1-bed ≈ 900 · 2-bed ≈ 1,150 · 3-bed+ ≈ 1,500 kWh), then adjusts for home type, people, and the usage drivers you select (A/C, electric heat, EV ≈ +300 kWh, pool ≈ +200, and more) — anchored to the ≈ 1,100 kWh/month statewide average[1]. Enter a real bill and we use that instead. Each plan's cost is modeled at that usage as all-in price — provider energy + base charges, minus usage credits, plus TDU delivery — the components a Texas Electricity Facts Label discloses at 500 / 1,000 / 2,000 kWh[2].

1

2

3

4
U.S. Dept. of Energy — appliance/EV/HVAC energy-use guidance behind the usage adjustments. energy.gov
